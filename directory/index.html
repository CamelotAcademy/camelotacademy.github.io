Eric Fossinger (eric@camelotacademy.org)

MAPP Teacher

Teaching since 2012; at Camelot since 2025

Education: BA in Anthropology and Philosophy at UNC-Chapel Hill, 2007; M.Ed. in Early Childhood Education of Children with and without Disabilities, 2016; Certification in Nature Based Education through Association of Nature-Based Educators (ANBE), 2025

Travel: Guatemala, Mexico, Bolivia, Dominican Republic, Belize, Ireland

More About Me: Hi, everybody! I am a career early educator who has worked with children ages 2 through 10 in many different settings. I came to early education almost by accident, when my Americorps program placed me in a Kindergarten classroom at a Boston turnaround school. I immediately fell in love with the unrestrained enthusiasm that kindergarteners bring to the world and I am inspired to match that enthusiasm everyday in my work with young people. I continued to work with children for the next 16 years, in private daycare cooperatives, residential programs, summer camps, and finally again in public school where I worked for 8 years as an assistant Kindergarten teacher. Over time I became disheartened by the increasing focus in public schools on academic readiness and skill practice at the expense of play and open-ended discovery. When my family and I moved to the triangle two years ago I resolved to take a break from teaching until I could find a program that valued the learning potential in free play and exploration. Luckily I discovered Camelot and the Nature Start program and was able to join the team this year as a teacher. When I am not working outdoors with our students I am likely playing in the river with my own kids, biking, running, or pursuing more “indoor” hobbies like board games or afro-brazilian drumming.

Katie Rebich (katie@camelotacademy.org)

Specialist Teacher: Theater

At Camelot since 2022

Katie Rebich (they/them) is a teaching artist, performer, and theater educator since 2009. With a background that spans classroom teaching and professional performance, their work lives at the intersection of play, creativity, and emotional connection. Their pedagogy emphasizes student-led discovery and social-emotional learning, grounded in a strong belief that curiosity and creativity are essential to meaningful education.

Drawing from their background in physical theater and contemporary clowning, Katie brings movement, humor, and emotional presence into the learning space. They formally trained at Aloft Circus Arts in Chicago and studied clown with Dody DiSanto and Giovanni Fusetti, grounding their teaching in Lecoq-based practices and a deep respect for the intelligence of children. They continue to perform locally with their company, StoryUp! Aerial Theater. You may even catch them on stilts with Paperhand Puppet Intervention!

Whether in the classroom or onstage, Katie is committed to creating joyful, inclusive spaces where curiosity thrives, learning is active, failure is welcomed, and every student has a place to shine.
