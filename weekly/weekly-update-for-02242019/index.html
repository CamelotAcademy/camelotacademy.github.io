Weekly Update for 02/24/2019

Sunday, February 24, 2019

Greetings!
  
Congrats to our FTC Robotics Team, who participated in the State Tournament in Asheville yesterday.   They won the First Place Innovate Award and were First Runner Up for both the Connect Award and the Inspire Awards!  What an exciting day!
 
 
Another highlight of this week was the Global Studies Certificate applicant interviews.  Nine passionate high school students supported their written applications during inspiring exchanges with our four faculty liaisons. It is very exciting to anticipate how this program will continue to grow in the years to come.
 
It is also exciting to note how many student initiatives are underway to make a difference in the world.  See below….
 
From Junior John Wang - Saturday, March 2:
Our school is again running a water station for the Florence Forth Road Race. All ages are welcome to participate, but students under the age of 12 need an adult to accompany them, or they can participate by creating signs for the water station during lunchtime at school.  This is a fun bonding volunteer opportunity and a great way for students to earn hours towards the Camelot-wide service-hour count that NHS is sponsoring, and for personal resume building.  Volunteers will need to be at the corner of Main St. and Morning Glory, near Golden Belt in downtown Durham, from 7:10-9:30 AM on Saturday, March 2nd.
To sign up to help, click here:  https://www.signupgenius.com/go/60b0a4ea9ad29abfd0-2019
 
From Senior Kira Arges - Thursday, March 7:
Empty Bowls is an annual fundraiser held by Urban Ministries of Durham.  All proceeds go to support the important work UMD does to feed, clothe and shelter the homeless in our community. Volunteers will help by bussing tables and assisting attendees with limited mobility. This opportunity is for students in 10th grade and above.  Sign up here:  https://www.signupgenius.com/go/70a0448a5ab29a3f58-empty
 
 
From Senior Dylan Nono – February 25 - March 22:
The students in the Global English class were strongly influenced by their recent read of The Kite Runner. Author Khaled Hosseini says “There are a lot of children in Afghanistan, but little childhood.”  The book focuses on the way people in Afghanistan are dying and suffering because of violence and conflict. Our class is taking the initiative to fundraise for the organization of Khaled Hosseini in order to help the children who are victims of all the violence created by the endless war in Afghanistan. This week, sanitized water bottles will be given to any student who is willing to take one home to collect change between now and Spring Break. The water bottles have slots in them which are made to easily insert coins. At the end of the collection, the homeroom that raises the most money will have the chance to be part of a pizza/doughnut party as a reward for their contribution. Thank you for your support and understanding!
