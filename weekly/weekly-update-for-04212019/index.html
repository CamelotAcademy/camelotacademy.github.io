Weekly Update for 04/21/2019

Sunday, April 21, 2019

Greetings!
 
I hope you are enjoying a nice long weekend with your family.
 
Last week we made some special memories at Camelot.  On Thursday, Ms. Charlotte orchestrated a wonderful Immersion Day for our students.  She had excellent assistance from the entire teaching team, many student presenters, and the Kamin Family, who contributed refreshments. Later that day, Ms. Jenny and Mr. Josh orchestrated a beautiful Upper School Dance, supported by numerous members of the staff.  Parents Gina Barrantes, Angela and Dougald Fountain, Adam Wenzlik, Kathryn Bailey and Carlos Feaster were instrumental to creating the elegant ambience and refreshments. Kudos to students David Madugu and Robert Sutcliffe for their great work as DJs.
 
Congratulations to our Robotics Teams and Coaches for the amazing experience at the FIRST World’s Competition in Houston last week.  The FTC Team finished 19th out of 160 teams and they were finalists for the Connect Award.  The Robofoxes presented and volunteered at the conference, and Junior Zach Ireland interviewed for a Dean’s List Scholarship.  Junior Heather Hartman won a $500 FRC Team Eagle Robotics Scholarship based on her essay about how FIRST inspires her. It was a whirlwind of memorable experiences; we are deeply grateful to the dedicated parent volunteers who make this happen and the phenomenal orchestration of it all by Coach Robyn.
 
Reminders/Announcements:
 
The in-school Talent Show is this Friday.  Students who wish to share a talent are encouraged to sign up to do so (sign-up sheet will be posted in the Big Hall on Tuesday).  Each entry is limited to three minutes and can include instrumental, vocal, dramatic or other forms of entertainment.  The Talent Show will be followed by our Spring Festival to be held at Forest Hills Park.  We will transport students to and from the park and provide a pizza lunch.  To accommodate the food and transportation, the pick up from Camelot is slightly later than originally published.
12:30 PM MAPP dismissal
12:45 Middle Grades and Upper School  dismissal
 
From NHS member Elizabeth Whetzel… DONATIONS TO END HUNGER STILL NEEDED:  At the annual Durham CROP Hunger Walk, the Camelot team walked nearly five miles to show support for ending world hunger. However, the problem isn’t solved yet. People in our community and around the world suffer from hunger and malnutrition every day. There is still time to make a difference by donating athttps://www.crophungerwalk.org/durhamnc/Team/View/98612/Camelot-Academy. Last year, the Camelot community donated $1700 and won the Golden Shoe Award for our contribution. Our goal for this year is $1500. Donate by clicking the link above to help us reach our goal and help families across the globe. Thank you for your contribution and support.
