Weekly Update for 08/25/2019

Sunday, August 25, 2019

Greetings!

 

We are BACK!  The faculty returned on Monday, August 12 and we had a tremendous first week of professional development, academic and classroom prep.  During our second week, we had 1:1 time for each of our MAPP students and each of our members of the Class of 2020.  Our Global Studies students came in for a day of planning orientation activities, and many of our new Middle and Upper School students were hosted by returning students in a ”Meet-and-Greet.“ In addition, many staff members took a Mental Health First Aid course, and we all continued intensive work on everything from Red Cross first aid, serving “2E“ (gifted - twice exceptional students), to classroom management and reflective practice.  The longer period of Teacher Work Days in the calendar this year has been put to excellent use.

 

Our faculty has several exciting new members.  In addition to Anna Buchanan (introduced in my last email), our MAPP and Middle Grades teams welcome Mr. Ab (AbduRabbWatkins).  Ab, who recently moved here from South Carolina, is a veteran teacher with teaching experience both in this country and in Indonesia. We also welcome Mr. Thomas Van Duyne to our team.  Thomas recently moved here from New York City and has taught in many kinds of interesting environments, including Audubon New York and the Rock Eagle 4-H Center in Georgia. Finally, Ms. Leah Wilhelm (Matt’s wife; mom of Lincoln and Maggie) extends her role in our family to oversee elective class scheduling, after-school and Optional Care programming and summer opportunities. Leah brings a wealth of talents to the mix, including many years of arts administration and instruction (dance and visual arts) in local public high schools. We will miss Ms. Liz and Ms. Frankie this year and wish them all good things in their new pursuits.  We will also miss Mr. Josh, who has decided to take a hiatus this school year to travel and pursue personal goals 

 

We look forward to introducing you to our newest faculty on Monday, at one of our Parent Orientation Sessions held throughout the day.  We have a new format (which includes potluck dishes - many from our featured summer read Deep Run Roots) and we will host small group discussions to share all the plans for this year with an emphasis on new classes, programming, procedures, etc.

 

Please note that the building will be closed on Tuesday, August 27. After a marathon day on Monday we want everyone to rest and recharge before the return of our students on Wednesday, August 28!

 

We are set for an amazing year!  We look forward to sharing it with you.

 

Best,

Thelma
