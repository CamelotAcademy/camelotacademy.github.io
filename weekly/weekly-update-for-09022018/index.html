Weekly Update for 09/02/2018

Sunday, September 02, 2018

Greetings and Happy September!
 
I hope this finds you enjoying a nice holiday weekend. 
 
This week was our first week of classes, replete with all of the settling-in orchestration - from people to furniture - that you might expect.  By Friday, things were starting to hum along.  During the morning, I delighted in Ms. Judith and Ms. Alison’s fun with the MAPP students, who were in rapt attention as they learned their first Swahili words in their study of World Languages.  Ms. Amy and I took advantage of the break in humidity to sit on the porch and catch up.  She was excited by the great work her Middle Grades students had already produced.  While we were talking, a group of Middle Grades students emerged to use the garden as a place to work on their assignments. After finishing with Amy, I stopped in to see Ms. Magda and she was bursting with excitement about the first meeting of her Globalization course.  All this before 9:45 AM - before I had even made it upstairs from Morning Meeting!  I have always loved the excitement of the first few school days; this year has already produced many lovely moments.
 
This week, we will have the excitement of the launch of classes at the farm site for students K-12. In addition, the pool will open for our swimmers and our multi-generational music group will launch.
 
Are you a parent musician?  Can you join us on Wednesdays from 4-5:30 PM??  Mr. Glenn’s multigenerational music group will meet this Wednesday for an exploratory session.  Please join students and teachers and come with your instruments/voices ready for some fun.
