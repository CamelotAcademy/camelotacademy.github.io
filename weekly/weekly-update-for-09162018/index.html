Weekly Update for 09/16/2018

Sunday, September 16, 2018

Greetings!
 
I hope this email finds you and yours safe, dry and well.  The teachers and I were able to carry on with our faculty retreat in spite of the weather, and we are deeply appreciative of your input to this important process. It is always an inspiration to witness the good work, creativity and collaboration in the quest of creating “a unique path” for each of our students.  I am humbled to work with the truly amazing people who are my colleagues on Team Camelot.  We look forward to sharing class details with you in our upcoming Parent Nights.
 
We are happy to welcome Wind Gao to our Camelot Family.  Wind has recently arrived from China and joins our Middle Grades English Language Learner students.
 
The coming week will have some weather impacts both in terms of our sports activities and our visits from college reps. Our home-based cross country meet, originally scheduled for this Tuesday, will be rescheduled.
 
This week begins the Friday Early Dismissal Option for Middle and Upper School Parents.  More info will be presented at Thursday’s Parent Night but the gist of it is that if your student does not need to stay for a make-up test, then pick-up between 2:30 PM and 2:45 PM is at your discretion.  For those wishing to remain at school, there will be an in-school study hall from 2:45-3:15 PM, followed by the usual dismissal routines. Triangle Chess will also offer a club for chess players of all experience levels.  Sign-up is for the entire semester (through mid-January), play is 2:30-3:30 PM and registration info is here: Click Here .  Please note that our hours of operation on Fridays are NOT abbreviated - we will be open until 5:30 PM, as usual.
 
Parent Nights (one of the required parent events):
... This Thursday for Upper School Parents ...
... Next Thursday (Sep. 27) for MAPP and Middle Grades Parents ...
 
During these sessions you will meet with each of your student’s teachers to learn general class info and expectations.  This information is critical to you getting the most out of your individual family conference, coming up the week of October 8th.  Thanks, in advance, for making these sessions a priority. Our collaboration is sure to maximize the school-year experience for your student.
