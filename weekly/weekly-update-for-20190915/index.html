Weekly Update for 09/15/2019

Sunday, September 15, 2019

Greetings!

 

It is hard to describe the feeling that came over me about 4:30 PM on Friday…

That was when we finished our retreat meetings - a particularly productive and collaborative few days.  Relief, joy, excited anticipation….although I’m not a fan of flying, there is an awesome feeling to be experienced when a plane leaves the ground and starts to rise above the Earth and you are reminded of the all the possibilities of a journey into the vast and beautiful world…and that is the feeling that overcame me on Friday afternoon as this school year truly became airborne.

 

What did we do during our retreat?

           

We discussed each and every student - the data, the anecdotal observations, the goals and the next steps. We exchanged ideas as to how to maximize each student’s talents and interests and how to address challenges. As a new faculty member remarked on Wednesday afternoon at the end of our first two hours, “Wow - this is intense!”

 

In addition to our Main Agenda of reviewing each student….

-         We debriefed our first Digital Citizenry lessons to Middle and Upper School and discussed best next steps

-         We finalized our school-wide September X Schedule plans - transportation, classes, rooms, protocols - it is a very ambitious 10 days and we are looking forward to some amazing things!

-         We nailed down details of the upcoming Middle Grades trip (October) to Piney Point

-         We fine-tuned the plans for Friday afternoons to maximize opportunities for 1:1 pullouts and other learning experiences requested by parents and students

-         We finalized plans for MAPP “specials” for the year - including their Digital Citizenship experience

 

Over the next two weeks we will be communicating and implementing the changes and adjustments that are the outcome of our retreat.  At our upcoming Parent Nights - MAPP (Sept 26) and Middle/Upper (Oct 10) - we will go through the class-level plans so that at our Individual Family Conferences (Oct 23-25) we can specifically focus on your student’s “unique path” for the year. 

 

We are deeply appreciative of the time our families allow us for this important and necessary reflective step that is critical to delivering on our school’s mission.  On behalf of our entire team - Thank you.
