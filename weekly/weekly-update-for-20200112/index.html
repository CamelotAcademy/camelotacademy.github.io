Way to go, RoboKnights!  The team competed in Farmville on Saturday and won the Motivate Award and the 2nd Place Inspire Award.  They will now advance to the NC FTC State Tournament!

 

 

Morning Meeting:

 

From time to time I will use this space to share some of what we discussed in Morning Meeting the past week.  As you may know, this start to our day is central to our community and we talk about everything from student and teacher kindnesses to world events.  This week we continued a discussion we started in 2019 about bird migration patterns.  Mr. Thomas and Ms. Lori are invaluable resources to these discussions.  Our conversation was inspired by this article https://www.newsobserver.com/living/article238366508.html about local changes in hummingbird patterns. Also, we had a Morning Meeting virtual “visit” with Camelot student Nithya Mohanan who is studying in India this year.  Students were able to ask her questions about her life in India and we were able to tell her about some of the highlights of this school year.  

 

Announcements:

 

The upcoming week is the last week of Semester 1.  Middle and Upper Students will take Math exams on Thursday morning and Foreign Language exams on Friday morning.  Students who do not take a class in one of those subject areas should not attend school during that exam session.  Please help your student prepare for exams by reviewing the material indicated on Exam Review Sheets, ensuring adequate rest and a good breakfast. Specifically, students who do not take Latin, Spanish, Mandarin or ELL should not attend school on Friday (it is an early dismissal day). 

 

Seeking Parent Service Career Speakers - for 30 minute rotations between 9 AM and 11 am and 1:15 PM and 3:15 PM on Tuesday and Wednesday, January 21 and 22.  You will be asked to present 4 times (30 minutes each) during one of these two-hour blocks to Middle and Upper School students eager to learn about different career paths.  If interested, please send me an email with your availability and topic.

 

10th and 11th grade students who took the PSAT may now view their scores online.  Students should log in to their College Board accounts for scores and interpretive information.  We will also be reviewing these scores with students later this month. 

 

Our students love to learn to cook and are looking for more opportunities to do so.  We are looking for commercial kitchen space that is close by with hourly rental at a reasonable price.  If you have any leads along those lines, please forward them to me.

 

Club Preview Week is the week of January 27.  In addition to our multi-generational band, we will be producing an original play written by our own Scott Mitchell with a multi-generational cast.  Here is a teaser:

Suppose the story of Hamlet unfolded in real time on American television (Hamlet interviewed on 'The Today Show'; Ophelia guesting on 'The View', Laertes competing with Hamlet on 'Fencing with the Stars').  Suppose further that every plot development is spun by partisan political factions (Royalist vs. Rebels) seeking to advance their views. You have just imagined the world of 'Hamlet: A Televised Tragedy'. We are planning a spring production for this original work and have 37 parts to cast (all the ones you'd expect: Hamlet, Horatio, Claudius, Gertrude, etc. plus a few you that are new to the canon: Oprah, Larry King, Wolf Blizter, Barbara Walters, Rachel Madow, Joe Garigola).Want to play?  Contact Scott Mitchell. All students, family members and faculty are encouraged to audition for this multi-generational production.
