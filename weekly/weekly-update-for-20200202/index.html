Weekly Update for 02/02/2020

Sunday, February 02, 2020

Greetings and Happy February!

 

Congratulations to senior Heather Hartman on her acceptance to Florida State University!

 

Morning Meeting Highlights:

Good News Notices:

- Jack Walquist-Evans - for exemplary math pace (he has already completed the equivalent of two full years!)

- Gavin Petelle- for earning a great score on a Social Studies quiz and giving his best even when he was feeling a bit sick

- The Swim Meet participants were each recognized for their records and accomplishments

- The Basketball team was recognized for a great season.  Travis and LM Fountain and David Madugu represented our school and the team at the All-Star game on Friday night

We also had extensive conversations prompted by student X-Schedule travels.  The Environmental and Global classes went to a local landfill.  The info gained ignited a bit of shock and desire for action - we were all surprised to learn that our area produces significantly MORE landfill contributions per person than the national average.  Stay tuned for a Camelot initiative to change our part of that equation.  Meanwhile, the Civics class returned from a four-day trip to Washington, DC.  Without a doubt, the highlight for the participants was the hour they spent watching the impeachment proceedings.  The glimpse of history-in-the-making was an experience they will always remember.

 

A special shout-out to the faculty and staff - most especially Ms. Jill and my sister Donna (who does our IT support remotely) - without whom the HUGE task of getting reports, grades and transcripts published on time could not happen.  The January report deadline is the most ambitious calendar event of the school year, requiring every staff member to “hit the mark” well and on time to pass the baton to the next “link” in the procedural chain.  There is always a unique obstacle or two of illness, weather, technical issues or such to make it all exciting.  I am moved to honor our team with a “virtual” standing ovation for the great job done this year.

 

To that end, we hope you have had the opportunity to carefully review the comments and grades on the Achieve portal.  The individual course templates will be mailed on Monday and provide a more detailed breakdown of exam performance and specific areas of mastery.  For families coming in for conferences this week, please prepare your thoughts and questions regarding this information and second semester plans.  Please also remember that STUDENTS IN GRADES 5-12 MUST BE IN ATTENDANCE at their conference. 

 

ANNOUNCEMENTS:

The Global Studies Certificate Program is now accepting applications from students who are interested in joining us in the next school year (2020-2021). This program is for students entering 10th and 11th grade next year who want to engage in global issues and develop skills that are essential for global citizenship. The application period closes on Tuesday, February 18th.  All applicants will have a personal interview on Friday, February 28th.  Information and applications are available at camelotacademy.org/global-studies.  Questions can be directed to Ms. Charlotte at charlotte@camelotacademy.org.
