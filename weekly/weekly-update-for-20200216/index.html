Weekly Update for 02/16/2020

Sunday, February 16, 2020

Greetings!

 

We had a great week with lift-off for a new slate of elective classes and after-school clubs.  From woodworking to yoga, leadership to bookbinding, things are bustling.  The yearbook crew is in full swing documenting our activities, and the music groups that will perform at Evening of the Arts have begun rehearsal.

 

CONGRATS to the Battle of the Books Team and Coach Amy.  The team won its second match (vs. Immaculata School). This dedicated group of students -  who practice at 7:30 AM on Tuesdays - have had an incredible season. 

 

Kudos to NHS and the magnificent management of Ms. Amy (same person as Coach Amy noted above) who orchestrated a fun and learning-infused Science Night for our younger students. A special nod to NHS Secretary Senior Robert Sutcliffe who was the lead on this event.

 

In Morning Meeting, we cheered about the farm’s latest addition, a six-month-old donkey named Peanut. Meanwhile, Mr. Thomas and a group of student helpers have launched a school-wide composting project.  We are tracking our progress and talking about it regularly.  We look forward to sharing updates with you.

 

REMINDERS:

If you have a survey from your conference, please complete and return it at your earliest convenience.

This Friday is Picture Day for SPORTS TEAMS, absentee, retakes and GRADUATING SENIORS.  Please pay careful attention to the details provided by Ms. Wendy in the email that went home on Thursday, Feb 13.

We could use a few more ADULT ACTORS for the play that Mr. Scott wrote and is producing with help from Mr. Ab.  Please send an email to scott@camelotacademy.org to learn more.

A bird walk has been scheduled at the Camelot Farm for Saturday, March 14.  Sign up here:

https://www.signupgenius.com/go/10c084ca4a622a1fa7-march

 

ANNOUNCEMENT:

If your student is in an off-campus E class, please consider arriving for pick-up NO EARLIER than 3:20 PM. This provides time for students to return to campus and pack up thoughtfully.  Middle and Upper School students are not charged for after-school until 3:40 PM, so there is plenty of time for parents to arrive a bit later and spend less time waiting.
