Weekly Update for 03/22/2020

Sunday, March 22, 2020

Greetings!

 

Happy Spring!  I hope you have had occasion to marvel at the bursting forth happening in nature right now.

 

Kudos to Senior Wyatt Pell, who was accepted to Elon University with a 24 K Merit Scholarship.

 

A Big Hurrah to Ms. Jill and my sister Donna for persevering to see that Mid Semester grades and comments went out on schedule.  Huge thanks to Ms. Wendy and Mr. Scott for making sure all other office business stayed on track this week.

 

Although we are on Spring Break, I wanted to send a brief message of thanks for the school-home collaboration that made for an amazing first week of virtual school.  I know our team learned a lot and there were many highlights - from SR MAPP dancing together (virtually) to Chemistry class with Ms. Lori using chocolates and toothpicks to demonstrate chemical structures.  From the laptop at my kitchen table I am able to sign in and out of each virtual room, say hello,  and observe what teachers and students are working on.  In addition, my email inbox is a flood of copies of messages from students submitting work and questions. It is akin to being in the Big Hall during class time and hearing snippets from each of the classrooms!

 

I love and admire these teachers and staff members who have worked AROUND THE CLOCK, completed Mid Semester paperwork, learned new teaching platforms, responded to 100’s of emails, met with me (virtually) daily - all with enthusiasm, care and professionalism. More than once while watching them teach virtually I became overwhelmed with emotion at the beauty of their outpouring of dedication to our students and our work together.  I am glad for them to have the upcoming week for a much needed few days to rest and regroup and tend to their own households and loved ones.

 

Meanwhile, we have never felt more connected to you - the parents and guardians.  Many of you have participated in MAPP Morning Meetings, others have been right on the other side of email to help a student sign on or troubleshoot a technical difficulty (I believe we have all mastered the ways to “turn in” work now).  In addition to all of this help, parent outpouring of notes of appreciation has been a lovely and unexpected occurrence.  I opened and closed each of our faculty meetings this week by reading notes, anecdotes - and even a POEM! - written to the staff in appreciation for making this unprecedented transition a supported one - both emotionally and academically - for our dear students.

 

The timing of much about our school calendar this year has been serendipitous to keeping student learning on track during this time.  As you know, we had our annual standardized testing especially early, we conducted all of our family conferences and our move to virtual was the day after the mid-semester reporting period!  Even the timing of our faculty retreat - after four days of virtual instruction - allowed us to maximize our planning because it was based on the real experience of implementation.

 

My Update next Sunday will include more details of our ongoing virtual implementation.  Until then, I hope you find many bright spots in each day.

 

Stay safe and well,

 

Thelma
