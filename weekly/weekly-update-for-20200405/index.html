Weekly Update for 04/05/2020

Sunday, Month 05, 2020

Greetings and Happy April!

 

We enjoyed a good week of settling into our virtual routines.  On Thursday, I had an oddly quiet day of too much paperwork and not enough Camelot Family.  I became aware that I missed the sounds of children on the playground and of lockers slamming every hour to keep track of time.  In a tech-stupor, I asked Alexa to play “Playground Sounds” for me - and she did!  It was a very poor substitute for the real thing and I vowed to start Friday by dropping into several classrooms.  That was the answer!  In class on Friday I saw Ms. Krista demonstrating a somersault to a MAPP class and I happened upon a “guest speaker” in one of Mr. Matt’s history classes talking about the way the pandemic was impacting his business.  My Lesson Learned for the week is that the thousands of impromptu interactions we all have daily (verbal and nonverbal) are an important part of our life experience and we must be mindful of tending to them while we are practicing “social distancing.”  To that end, the teachers are doing a great job of incorporating classroom discussions and small working groups into their in-person routines - to allow group dynamic times.  One teacher reported to me that she has a student who signs on early every day just to chat while she does household routines before starting class - we are finding our way.

 

You, the parents and guardians, are our heroes.  As you know, one of Camelot’s three guiding principles is “Parents as Team Members” and this time together is the High Water Mark of that tenet. I am on the receiving end of copies of hundreds of messages each week that are sent by teachers as outreaches to parents to help keep a student on course.  I am also copied on all of the replies.  “Got it!,” “We are on it!”  etc., etc. - my inbox is testimony to all of the collaboration between school and home that is working behind the scenes to make this experience worthwhile and balanced for our students and their families.  We don’t take for granted all of the new variables each household is juggling and we encourage you to keep in dialogue with us to help us find the right supportive balance point for your family.
