Weekly Update for 05/03/2020

Sunday, May 03, 2020

Greetings and Happy May!

 

Congratulations to the NHS officers elected for service in the upcoming school year:

President:  Elizabeth Whetzel

Vice President At Large: Trent Porter

Vice President for Communications:  Anna Bailey

Treasurer: LM Fountain

Secretary: Nelle Poehlman

 

 

We have a big week coming up with a few fun SURPRISES!

 

In honor of our amazing teachers and National Teacher Appreciation Day on TUESDAY - please join me in giving the team a one day “holiday” from all scheduled online classes!  We extend this appreciation to YOU -  our parent/guardian “Teacher Partners” -  who have been doing an amazing job of working collaboratively with us from home.  There has never been a year more perfect for this celebration and the gift of TIME is one we can all share. As you are aware, Camelot has not deviated from our published calendar during the transition to virtual-mode and now we find ourselves with a couple of “extra” days in May that were originally going to be used for on-campus special programming.  We have solved this by extending the “regular” schedule by one day (Monday - which also provided an extra weekend - giving students more time to finish assignments) and then by taking a single day “off” (Tuesday). These adjustments will ensure that the end of month virtual programming will flow well. This “rejigging” seems a smart use of time and allows for a day of transition for all - teachers, students and parents - between the end of the “Regular” and start of the “X” Schedule.

 

Please join me in celebrating Team Camelot (which includes YOU -  our Parents/Guardians) with the Tuesday “Day of Rest”!

 

 

Here’s how this all fits together…

Monday:

Last day of classes on the “regular” schedule for Middle and Upper School students. 

MAPP schedule will stay the same throughout the remainder of the school year. They will have additional extension activities related to the farm.

 

11:40 Middle and Upper Grades Morning Meeting - IMPORTANT - at this meeting students will receive and have explained their individual schedules for the upcoming “X” Days.  The link to the meeting will go out through the Morning Meeting Google Classroom on Monday Morning.  All C Period teachers will ensure their classes sign on.  The meeting will end by Noon.

 

MONDAY E Period will have a unique slate of classes - ALL Tu/Th E Period academic classes will meet (High School Latin, Spanish 3 and one section of English). IN ADDITION, we will run the last Livestream from the Farm for the school year at 2 PM (featuring a property tour and project summary by Mr. Thomas). Ms. Judith will run the last session of her drawing elective and we will run the 1:45-2:45 study hall by request.

 

 

Tuesday:

 

Teacher Appreciation Day - a day off for ALL!

 

No virtual class meetings.

Parents and students have access, if so desired, to Google Classroom materials.

 

 

Wednesday:

 

Our Well-Rested Team rolls out X Schedule!

 

-X Schedule begins for Middle and Upper School students. 

 

PARENTS PLEASE NOTE:

- Middle/Upper classes run from 8:30 AM to 3:15 PM. 

-No student has classes during that entire time period but most students do have a class that runs until 3:15 PM.

-Please also note that math classes, Spanish classes, high school Latin and Mandarin classes continue to meet during X Schedule for a full hour each day.  (Some students have these classes at a different time, but with the same teacher.)

-Students are familiar with these changes from earlier in the year and we will review it all at the Morning Meeting on Monday and then be on standby with the Virtual Classroom Hotline for incoming text messages about any confusion on Wednesday (text 919 886-5792).

 

X Schedule done virtually is a true testimony to the creativity and innovation of our teachers. Students will have guest speakers, virtual field trips, simulations, demonstrations and much more in every discipline from Civics and Environmental Science to rock climbing and cooking. Although these classes are “scheduled” for just under four hours a day, the time will be broken up into a beginning and end of class check-in/lesson with the instructor and will include a lunch break and time for small group and independent work. MAPP students will round out their farm experiences for the year with lessons that will include their own “livestreams” from the farm.

 

Upcoming - please stay-tuned to my emails as we nail down the details:

 

-         AP Exams

 

-         Math and Foreign Language Exams

 

-         Graduation - an all-school event (done in a unique-to-the-times format)

 

 

AND a parent survey about some summer options we are exploring.

 

 

Thanks for your attention to all of the details!  We are looking forward to sharing many exciting learning adventures and to together creating some new twists on old traditions to ring out the school year!

 

I hope your day includes some time to enjoy the sunshine.

 

 

Best,

Thelma
