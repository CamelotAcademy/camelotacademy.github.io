Weekly Update for 10/04/2020

Sunday, October 04, 2020

Greetings and Happy October!

 

What amazing fall weather we are having!  I hope you are finding ways to enjoy it. 

 

Our students and teachers had another week of scaling new frontiers. Among my favorite moments was Ms. Lori’s biology class, taught on the Camelot roof, with lab partner pairings of one on-campus student with one virtual student. It worked great and we have pictures of it all!

 

We wrapped up Module 2 on Friday and tomorrow begins Module 3 which includes new classes for many Upper School students as part of the C Rotation.

 

We welcome some new part-time faculty members this week:

 

“Sasha” (Aleksandra) Bursac will be teaching MAPP and Middle Grades Music. Sasha was born and raised in Serbia and lived in the Netherlands before moving to the US.  She has taught students of all ages both in the classroom and in her private music studio.

 

Erin Sullivan - will be teaching Middle and Upper School performing arts. Erin spent 18 years in New York City as a professional singer, actor, dancer, director, producer and published author.  In addition to teaching privately and in master classes she has consulted on projects with many area high schools since moving to Durham a year and a half ago.

 

Robin Brown Pipkins - will be one of our Upper School arts instructors. Robin has over 10 years of experience working with young people through the arts in local camps, schools and recreation centers. Robin is also an active volunteer in many organizations that support families and children.

 

 

In addition to these exciting additions to our community, the next two weeks of Module 3 have a lot in store:

 

MAPP students - begin to add specials to their schedules AND Jr MAPP students will be the first to ride a Camelot bus this school year on their first of weekly trips to the farm!

 

MIDDLE GRADES students - welcome Erin Sullivan for their specials classes this module.  Hybrid Schedule students will continue to come to campus on the same days as last module.  Virtual students continue to log in for “Specials” classes at the same times - but for a new class.

 

UPPER SCHOOL students - start their C Rotation classes (a change in the “long-block” class for all but those enrolled in IPS/Foundations, who have another two weeks of those classes).  All Upper School parents received an email last week from the C Class instructor with details about plans for on-campus rotations.
