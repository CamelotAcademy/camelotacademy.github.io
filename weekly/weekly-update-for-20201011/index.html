Weekly Update for 10/11/2020

Sunday, October 11, 2020

Greetings!

 

I hope you are well.

 

We are deeply appreciative of current weather patterns - needed rain on Saturday-Monday, sunshine Tuesday-Friday! 

 

I had the good fortune to greet students at the farm on both Wednesday and Thursday mornings last week.  A highlight was Mr. Matt’s US History class - taught in and around the historic cabin that we moved to the property a couple of years ago.  Mr. Matt “had a moment” when he realized the cabin dated to the era the class was studying (>200 years ago) and I had a moment when I realized two of the students in the class (and seated in the cabin) had been central to the process of dismantling and documenting the move!  I can’t think of a more lovely way to re-gather our Camelot Family than around opportunities such as this.

 

Congratulations to senior Nina Elvin on her acceptance to the National Honor Society.  Nina was one of the students referenced above who was central in the cabin move and now she joins our NHS chapter, which provides us with many opportunities for community engagement.

 

Our National Honor Society students are hard at work re-imagining Camelot traditions for current times. 

Here are some announcements about upcoming NHS activities:

 

The Walk is virtual, but the need could not be more real!

The CROP Hunger Walk raises money to fight hunger in both our local community and around the globe. Due to the events of last year, the Spring CROP Walks were down about $1 million dollars, in a time when these donations couldn’t be more necessary. As such, the NHS has decided to move our participation date forward to Oct. 25. In the past, Camelot has been one of the biggest school contributors, contributing over $1,700 and winning the Gold or Silver Shoe award several years in a row. If you are interested in supporting the efforts of the CROP Hunger Walk you can donate and/or sign up as a walker on the Camelot team at:

https://www.crophungerwalk.org/durhamnc/camelotacademy

 

 

It’s time for our annual Socktober Event!

For Socktober, we collect as many socks as we can and donate them to Urban Ministries. Socks are often overlooked when people donate to the homeless, while being one of the most requested items, and we would like to raise awareness to that and do what we can to help. If you would like to participate, you will be part of a national community service project that can help families all across the United States. Boxes labelled by Homeroom are on the porch near the front door. The Homeroom with the most socks will get a prize at the end of the month. Feel free to drop off clean, unworn socks anytime this month!
