Weekly Update for 10/18/2020

Sunday, October 18, 2020

Greetings!

 

Module 3 is a wrap and we are looking forward to meeting with each of our Camelot families in the coming week of conferences.  Last week had many bright spots, including the Middle Graders’ enthusiasm and imagination in their Theater Arts class. I was captivated to watch their faces as they listened to some famous show-tunes.  You could almost SEE their imaginations at work and their attention to every nuance was a joy to behold.

 

 

REMINDERS:

 

Family Conferences:

On Friday, you should have received a confirmation email from Jill Pitino with the final conference schedule and detailed instructions. Please note carefully when your appointment is and how to link to it.  Please sign on with your thoughts and questions prepared (and your student, if Middle or Upper School!). Please be prompt and expect to be in the Virtual Waiting Room until the Conference Lead starts your meeting. The teaching team will share pre-test results and Quarter One progress - as well as academic plans for the year. (If you have connection issues, please TEXT the Hotline 919-886-5792)

 

These two NHS initiatives are counting on our support:

 

The CROP Hunger Walk raises money to fight hunger in both our local community and around the globe. Due to the events of last year, the Spring CROP Walks were down about $1 million dollars, in a time when these donations couldn’t be more necessary. As such, the NHS has decided to move our participation date forward to Sunday, Oct. 25. In the past, Camelot has been one of the biggest school contributors, contributing over $1,700 and winning the Gold or Silver Shoe award several years in a row. If you are interested in supporting the efforts of the VIRTUAL CROP Hunger Walk, you can donate and/or sign up as a walker on the Camelot team at: https://www.crophungerwalk.org/durhamnc/camelotacademy

 

 

It’s time for our annual Socktober event!  For Socktober, we collect as many socks as we can and donate them to Urban Ministries. Socks are often overlooked when people donate to the homeless, while being one of the most requested items, and we would like to raise awareness to that and do what we can to help. If you would like to participate, you will be part of a national community service project that can help families all across the United States. Boxes labelled by Homeroom are on the porch near the front door. The Homeroom with the most socks will get a prize at the end of the month. Feel free to drop of clean, unworn socks anytime this month
