Weekly Update for 11/08/2020

Sunday, November 08, 2020

Greetings!

 

I hope you and yours are well and enjoying the fall colors. 

 

This is a particularly content-filled edition of the Update.  Please take a few minutes to review the info carefully.

 

Thank you for your input on the Q2 Modality Sign-up.  As always, your feedback is invaluable to our process.  As we forge ahead into Quarter 2, here is a quick look back at Q1 highlights:

 

-         Our teachers led a successful student orientation, and academic review and pretesting module - all newly envisioned

-         They established robust virtual learning classrooms - utilizing many new platforms

-         We rolled out hybrid instruction - establishing outdoor learning protocols at both the farm and main campus, with resumed bus transport between the two

-         We hired and trained eight new part time staff members to assist with the delivery of individualized instruction with small onsite cohorts

-         We re-imagined and executed parent orientation, family conferences and social events (driveway parties)

-         We launched our first all-virtual after school club - now expanding to a multi-generational virtual option (see below for More Info)

-         We created a “unique path” as our school’s approach to reopening that prioritized:

--the safety and comfort-level of all stakeholders

--the highest standards of upholding the Camelot instructional models of individualized, mastery-based education with parents as team members

--an emphasis on arts and wellness for our in-person time

--the cohesion of our community - across all modalities

--a preparedness to maintain all of these factors - irrespective of modality of instruction

-         Our staff practiced and modeled RESILIENCE for ourselves and our students - finding ways to draw upon our individual strengths and talents and the power of community to navigate challenges

 

Tomorrow begins Quarter 2 …

…with a backdrop of holidays, travel and finding creative ways to best connect to our loved ones.  At Camelot, we look forward to:

            - Reimagining Academic Fair (see below for More Info) in a way that showcases student work, celebrates the Class of ‘21 and gives parents an opportunity to socialize

- Introducing some indoor instruction - with MAPP and lab sciences leading the way

- Continuing to increase MAPP time on campus

- Adding both virtual and hybrid club options - including a yearbook club

- Assisting interested families with expanded opportunities to form home learning pods to enhance the virtual or hybrid learning experience

Academic Fair - Message from English Dept. - Amy Cummings

Academic Fair is an important tradition for the Camelot Family, and we have a plan to preserve many of the key features of the event - and add some new features -  during the next few weeks.  A letter with more details will be sent later this week, but dates of note include:   

 

Friday, November 20 - 9am - 12pm - Upper School and Middle Grades students will present their research projects to peers and teachers in Zoom rooms in the morning (no regular classes).  It will be a half day of school for these students.

 

December 7, 8, 9 and 10;  7-8pm -  All parents should plan to attend at least one evening of student presentations.  Each night will feature 4-6 selected Upper School projects on a theme (for example, Science, Health, Technology, Social Science, Arts and Culture) in one Zoom Room, and Middle Grades projects in another. 

 

MAPP students should consider watching some Middle Grades presentations, and Middle Graders should plan to watch Upper School presentations that week. 

 

Additionally, all Upper School presentations will be recorded and made available to Camelot families on a private website beginning on December 11.  Parents can see their own students’ presentations, and the community can learn from as many of these projects as they choose to watch. 

 

The topics this year are incredibly varied and unique!  We will all learn from this sharing of research. 

 

Please look for more information later this week!

 

 

 

Message from Cinema Club Facilitator Raleigh Gardiner:

 

Cinema Club - now expanding to Multi-generational - parents and guardians - please join us!

 

We started in the 20s, and now we're traveling to the 30s in Cinema Club! This week, we will be watching Duck Soup (1933), a film starring the fabulous Marx Brothers! This classic, vaudevillian comedy is full of twists and turns, slapstick, and truly developed comic timing. This movie is truly classic-- its comedic and visual styles make it a revolutionary film. It is as funny today as it was in 1933!

 

We will be meeting on Kast on Monday, November 9th at 4pm, where I will introduce the film and we will watch a trailer together. Then, students will be directed to a Cinema Club youtube channel, where the movie will be pre-rented, and students will watch on their own. I will keep our Kast channel open throughout the movie, so we can continue to chat about the film as we're watching it.

 

We will reconvene to discuss Duck Soup on Tuesday November 10th at 4pm on Zoom!

 

Hope to see you all there!
