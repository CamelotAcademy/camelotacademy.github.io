Weekly Update for 11/15/2020

Sunday, November 15, 2020

Greetings!

 

I hope this email finds you and yours doing well and looking forward to the upcoming Thanksgiving holiday.

 

The week before Thanksgiving is always a highlight at Camelot as it is time for the Academic Fair - the culmination of weeks of student research and work. Thanks to our English teachers (Amy Cummings, Tom Mitchell, Erin Grady and Michelle Banal) and the support of all other staff members, we have a re-envisioned format that will span into the post-Thanksgiving weeks with showcases of work over multiple days.  Of course, parent support of this process is always instrumental to its success - clearly this year, more than ever.  Please see all the details in the email sent by Wendy on Friday (Nov 13) and note that email REQUIRES YOUR RESPONSE.

 

Huge thanks to Jill Pitino, my sister Donna and the entire teaching team for their efforts in producing Mid-Semester Reports.  As with pretty much everything this year, this included reformatting of our processes - check that off the list of protocols to figure out!  Every family should have received an email from Ms. Jill on Friday announcing your access to mid semester info via the Achieve portal. Please note the message in the cover letter - these are NOT officially recorded grades - they are simply a “snapshot” of a point in time at the midway mark of the semester. In addition to that whole school email, MAPP reports and Mid Semester Warnings were sent in separate emails from Jill on Friday afternoon. If your student received a Mid-Semester Warning, please note that requires an email response so we know you are in receipt. 

 

I’ve received some messages from the parents of Hybrid Schedule Students who intend for their students to stay virtual this week to decrease the risk of exposure ahead of family/travel holiday plans. We are happy to coordinate these requests - we ask that you notify us of these intentions by Monday (tomorrow) so we can properly staff and prepare lessons.  All Middle Grades Hybrid Students and their household members will continue to stay virtual this week in response to a possible COVID exposure within their pod on November 4.  To date, everyone remains well. If there is any change in status, I will immediately reach out to those who might be impacted - including over the holiday break. Meanwhile, we continue to be grateful for the use of our outdoor learning spaces with MAPP and Upper School hybrid schedule students.
