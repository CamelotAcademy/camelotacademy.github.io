Weekly Update for 11/29/2020

Sunday, November 29, 2020

Greetings! 

 

I hope you and yours enjoyed a lovely Thanksgiving. 

 

You may remember a few weeks ago I wrote about starting to send letters of recommendation for the Class of ‘21 - and humming Pomp and Circumstance - well, here come the first responses!

 

Congratulations to the following seniors on their college acceptances:

 

Nina Elvin - Tulane University with a 32K  Leadership Award

Trent Porter -  Baylor University with a 92K President’s Scholarship and University of Georgia

Ryan Rusconi-Warner - Clemson University

 

Also, congrats to all of our Middle Grades and Upper School students for their great work on Academic Fair research projects.  Kudos to our English teachers (Amy Cummings, Erin Grady, Tom Mitchell, Magda Sledzik Sims and Michelle Banal) for their tireless work coaching students through the process. We can’t wait to share it all with parents next week! (See detailed info below - under “Upcoming”)

 

Announcements:

MONDAY at 8:15 AM - all Upper School students attend Morning Meeting to review their Rotation A (English class) schedules.

 

Cinema Club will not meet this week but look for a survey to guide the choice of upcoming films and best days/times.

 

Reminders:

We are “all virtual” this week - all classes, all grade levels

 

Upper School English books for all classes, EXCEPT Ms. Erin’s morning class, were left on the porch for pick-up before break.  Please be sure to get yours in the next day.

 

Middle Grades students - ALL - have art materials to be picked up from the porch before their first specials class of the week.

            8:15 AM - Homeroom

Middle Grades - Art with Ms. Leah

- connect through the Specials Classroom

Upcoming:

 

Academic Fair presentations for parents 7- 8 PM, each evening, as follows:

 

December 7 - Science, Health and Technology

December 8 - History and Politics in one Zoom room;  Middle Grades Cohort A in another

December 9 - Social Science in one Zoom room;  Middle Grades Cohort B in another

December 10 - Arts & Culture in one Zoom room;  Middle Grades Cohort C in another

 

-         All parents should plan to attend at least one evening of student presentations, as attendance at the Academic Fair is a required parent event.  All are welcome to attend any of the options.

 

-         MAPP parents, please visit a Middle Grades room (perhaps with your child, so they can see what’s coming), or pick an Upper School theme that interests you.  You will learn something new, and you will witness what your child’s Academic Fair future holds!

 

-         Each Upper School event night will feature 4-6 selected Upper School projects organized by  theme, in one Zoom Room.  There will be time for questions and discussion after each presentation. 

 

-         On Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday, all Middle Graders will present shorter presentations in another Zoom room, organized by cohort (one cohort per night). 

 

-         Seniors will emcee each night, so seniors may be asked to be present multiple nights.  Mark your calendars, but we can also work around their schedules.  Seniors will meet with Mr. Thomas during the week of Nov. 30-Dec. 4 to prepare.

 

-         Middle Graders are encouraged to attend Upper School presentations on the evenings they are not presenting.

 

 

December 21- January 4 – School Closed for Winter Break

 

I hope you have a great week.

 

 

Best,

Thelma
