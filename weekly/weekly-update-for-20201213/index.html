CROP WALK GOLDEN SNEAKER AWARD:  Thanks to your generosity, Camelot Academy has won the Golden Sneaker Award for raising the most money of any Durham school for the CROP Walk this year.  Elizabeth Whetzel, NHS President, accepted this award in front of the Camelot building last weekend.  A video of this acceptance was presented at a virtual awards ceremony on Thursday evening.  Here is a link to the video.  Local recipients of the funds are featured at the beginning of the ceremony, and Elizabeth and Ms. Amy (NHS Advisor) can be seen beginning at about 50:00 minutes:  https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCLZwqE8iPC275Jcwnv8cBRA.

 

THANK YOU, CAMELOT PARENTS AND FAMILY MEMBERS

We had a great turnout to each and every one of the seven Academic Fair evening sessions. It was a joy to watch members of the Class of ‘21 serve as hosts and to learn so much from the huge variety of interests represented by our Middle Grades and Upper School presenters.  I heard more than one student comment on the great parent questions and interest - it got my wheels spinning in terms of future events! Special thanks to Mr. Thomas for taking the lead on moderator training and to Mr. Irfan for his invaluable help with producing the recordings of each and every student presentation.  Please look for an email this week with a link to the site on which all of this great work will be on display for you to enjoy.

 

REMINDERS:

 

December Service Saturday sponsored by the NHS...

 

This December, the Camelot National Honor Society is continuing our Service Saturdays initiative. Thank you to everyone who picked up trash last month. This month, we are encouraging members of the Camelot community to donate a toy to the Toys for Tots program. For those unfamiliar with the great work of Toys for Tots, according to its website, during the holiday season, “Coordinators, with the assistance of local social welfare agencies, church groups, and other local community agencies, distribute the toys to the less fortunate children of the community.” This year more than ever, it would be amazing to brighten the holiday season for less fortunate children. Due to the pandemic, Toys for Tots is receiving fewer toys than in previous years. At the link below, you have the option of picking out a toy and dropping it in a virtual toy box to purchase - contact free! The NHS hopes you have a happy holiday season!

https://toysfortotsbox.com/?utm_source=ToysforTotsFoundation&utm_medium=Website_DonateAToy&utm_campaign=ToysforTots_from_TFT
