Weekly Update for 01/17/2021

Sunday, January 17, 2021

Greetings!

 

I hope this finds you in the midst of enjoying the long holiday weekend.

 

As we enter the last nine days of the First Semester, students and teachers are engaged in demonstrating and documenting all of the learning that has taken place so far this year.  It has been heartwarming to hear MAPP teachers exclaim with delight the huge gains their students have posted in reading and math.  At the older grade levels, both Mr. Thomas and Ms. Lori excitedly invited me to join their classes this week to witness student accomplishments.  In the Environmental Engineering class, students are working to design (and later build) a bioswale to take the playground excavation work to the next level.  In Biology class, the students presented their creative depictions of the parts and workings of a cell that included a humorous analogy to the sections of an orchestra and the composition of an original song. The joy of the Camelot “classroom” is in excellent hands with this amazing instructional team.

 

Speaking of this amazing team, as we prepare to resume on-campus hybrid schedule instruction this week teachers have been flexible and creative in preparing to “meet students where they are.”  At least half of our hybrid schedule families are choosing to stay virtual a bit longer and teachers have been creating dual track plans to navigate this passage in a way that takes into account the needs of all.  The dedication of this team to the well-being of all members of our family continues to be a great inspiration.  Here’s the on-campus rotation plan for this week:

 

-         JR MAPP students have a unique schedule this week based on parent preferences for format.  Please refer to the email sent by Ms. Rita on Saturday. 

-         SR MAPP students are virtual this week to allow Mr. Ab to stay at the helm until his son is born (sometime this week!). 

-         Middle Grades students who have indicated the desire to come to campus will do so on Thursday or Friday, per their usual Specials schedule.

-         Upper School students are meeting with their D Rotation teachers virtually on Tuesday and then will decide as a class their preferences for on-campus instruction during this module.

As we resume on-campus rotations, our ability to continue and to expand this format is dependent upon all of us adhering to the highest level of cooperation with CDC and NCDHHS recommendations, most especially during this period of very high metrics of community spread. 

Those recommendations include not coming to campus if anyone in the household:

-         has any symptoms that can be associated with COVID,

-         has had a potential exposure to COVID

-         has tested positive for COVID since the start of Winter Break (please reach out to me to coordinate return protocols), or

-         has traveled outside the area in the last 10 days, or 7 days with a negative PCR test.

 

I’m happy to coordinate these or any COVID-related circumstances with you via email or phone.  No two scenarios are the same, but working together with prompt and full communication is the best strategy for keeping everyone well and keeping learning uninterrupted.  Thank you - many times over - for the beautiful spirit with which everyone has continued to take care of our Camelot Family.
