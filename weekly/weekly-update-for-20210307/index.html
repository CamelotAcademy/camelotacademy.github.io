Florence Forth Race - NHS Report from LM Fountain

Yesterday, Camelot's volunteers got up early and worked the Florence Forth race to support the Autoimmune Encephalitis Alliance. The volunteers set up water and directed the runners along the course. This year was different than previous years due to the course change. What did not change was the weather. Freezing and socially distanced, we persevered. Everyone worked hard and had a great time.

 

Kudos to Ms. Jill - who has once again coordinated a school-wide conference schedule that magically orchestrates the dance of so many to get the job done - and done very well.

Look for an email tomorrow (Monday) with your conference appointment time.

 

BEHIND THE SCENES - We had a traffic jam in the parking lot on Thursday morning - and we loved it! The numbers on campus have ramped up and it is very exciting.  The unique morning routines not only include COVID screenings but also the arrival of our Lamplighter - a young man named Tyler.  I like to tease Tyler by saying that if he puts this nearly-non-existent job title on his resume he will have something to talk about in interviews for the rest of his life!  Tyler sets us up each day by rolling out and lighting the heaters on the porch, as well as by opening all the windows and setting up the classrooms and interior spaces in a made-for-the-day configuration that reflects what classes are on site that day.  He comes back at 3:30 PM and puts it all away.  He is in a special category of Essential Workers!
