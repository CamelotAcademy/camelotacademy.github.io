BEHIND THE SCENES:

I’ve been enjoying and getting many ideas from Adam Grant’s newest work - Think Again.  The chapter on education has much to say about the importance of teaching and modeling the process of drafting and revision.  That practice has been especially alive and well this week as Ms. Robin and the dedicated yearbook team (Mattie Ramont, Thomas Smith, Nithya Mohanan and Dylan McGlinn) fine-tuned detail after detail in the final hours before sending the book to press.  We all owe them a debt of gratitude for their tireless efforts to produce a beautiful keepsake of this school year.  Meanwhile, Ms. Michelle has put in countless hours working with me to fine-tune the completely individualized Middle Grades schedules for Quarter 4.  Revision after revision, soliciting feedback and then regrouping  - she and I will be holding our breath this week as the magic (and possibly a few bumps!) of lifting the schedule “off the pages” takes place!

Reminders about on-campus protocols:

-         All students should be dropped off only after their cars enter our parking lot and all passengers are screened.  Please wait in your car and please do not drop off on side-streets.  If you arrive at a time when no one is on-site to screen, please call or text the “hotline” (919-886-5792) or the office (919-688-3040- no texts) and we will send someone to you.

-         Students in attendance for the full day should bring a bagged lunch and drink.  All students are encouraged to bring a water bottle.

-         No sandals or open-toed shoes on the playground or at the farm.  Farm shoes should be appropriate for muddy terrain! 

-         If you so choose, please provide your student with sunscreen or bug spray – preferably applied at home.

-         All student supplies for the day must fit in their backpacks.  We will not be using the lockers and all materials need to be brought home each day.

-         Official Movie Summary:

Angst has one goal; to start a global conversation and raise awareness around anxiety. Through candid interviews, the power of film is used to tell the stories of teens who discuss their anxiety and its impacts on their lives and relationships, as well as how they’ve found solutions and hope. The film also includes a special interview with Michael Phelps, a mental health advocate and one of the greatest athletes of all-time. In addition, the documentary provides discussions with mental health experts about the causes of anxiety and its sociological effects, along with the help, resources, and tools available.
