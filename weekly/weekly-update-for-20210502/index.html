Weekly Update for 05/02/2021

Sunday, May 02, 2021

Greetings!

 

This week is Teacher Appreciation Week! I think it is fair to say that all teachers everywhere have navigated one of the most challenging years of their careers.  Camelot teachers are no exception.  They have worked tirelessly to take care of our students’ needs - academically and emotionally - through each day’s challenges.  Like everyone, they have also had their own backdrop of personal and family well-being to balance.  I know you and your students share my great appreciation for this Camelot team - I encourage you to take this opportunity to remind the faculty how deeply they are cherished.

 

Congratulations to the following for their acceptance to Camelot’s National Honor Society:

Dominic Harding, Calvin Hinkle, Alex Mastropolo, Sam Pence, Thomas Smith, and Eli Terry.

 

Thanks to Ms. Amy for the hugely successful coordination of the Angst movie viewings and parent talk-back session.

 

Kudos to Mr. Thomas and Mr. Tom who spearheaded the Senior Gathering at the farm on Friday night. 

 

BEHIND THE SCENES:

Mr. Greg, Mr. Josh and Tyler are very busy readying the farm grounds for many upcoming school events.  The rotation of grazing animals to new spaces in order to prepare sections of the property for groups of people - all while juggling and protecting the evolving backdrop of nature (this week five baby geese hatched in the “wild”) - is quite a feat!

 

As noted above, many members of the Class of ‘21 and faculty gathered at the farm on Friday night to spend some time celebrating our journey together.  Among the highlights was the “one year late” initiation ceremony to NHS of seniors Nina Elvin, Trent Porter and Tully Daniel.  It was a very sweet, candlelit moment under the stars.

The CROP Walk is still happening!

The National Honor Society would like to ask you to sign up to walk the CROP Hunger Walk. Traditionally, the CROP Walk is a 5k representing the average distance people around the world have to walk to get water. Participating in the walk is a great way to raise awareness and shows support for the hungry and homeless. You can walk on your own or inspire your friends and family to walk with you, but first remember to Join The Team at: https://events.crophungerwalk.org/2021/team/camelot-academy

Additionally, you can donate/sponsor a teammate at the same link. All donations go to ending hunger in Durham and across the globe.

 

 

ANNOUNCE:

As you will note on our school calendar - ERB (Educational Record Bureau) testing is scheduled for this month.  The State of NC requires all private schools to administer nationally normed tests to all 3rd, 6th, 9th and 11th graders, as well as to students receiving certain state scholarships.

-         Our 3rd and 6th grade Hybrid-Schedule students will take these tests in person. 

-         6th grade Virtual-Schedule students - as well as ALL 9th and 11th graders - will take these tests remotely. (Note: 11th graders who have taken or registered to take the SAT or ACT are excused from ERB testing.)

-         Third graders will test on site on Fridays May 7, 14 and 21.

-         6th, 9th and 11th grade testing will be during the last two weeks of school.  We will post this schedule in my next Camelot Update.
