BEHIND THE SCENES:

Friday was a pretty amazing day.  The rain forced us to go to Plan B for recording the Middle Grades Evening of the Arts performances.  Thursday night, as the weather forecast continued to worsen, there was much concern about not wanting to disappoint the students who have been preparing for and looking forward to the first real school “event” in many months. Thanks to a brainstorm of Mr. Irfan and the all-hands-on-deck pitching-in of our arts teachers - Ms. Leah, Ms. Erin S, and Ms. Sasha - the parking lot was transformed into a soundstage.  Mr. Carlos and Tyler moved vehicles, tents, microphones - and then the parents arrived - in the rain - to watch and cheer the students on.  Outpourings of support as were peppered throughout the day are too inspirational for words.  The best part - the students laughed, rolled with the changes and showcased their work with winning attitudes that allowed all of the adults a deep sigh of gratitude that we had, indeed, succeeded in helping the students create a lovely memory.  As if those five hours of teamwork and trouble-shooting weren’t enough, the teaching team and staff packed up the show and were able to produce the Upper School performances, according to Plan A, at the beautiful Forest Theater. An amazing lineup of student accomplishments were shared to the backdrop of a setting sun. In the end, a day for all of our memory books.  Thank you, one and all - we look forward to sharing the recorded performances with you very soon.

 

REMIND:

Families have been doing a great job of working with us on communication about travel, exposures and illnesses to support our efforts to run our final weeks with a minimal of COVID-related interruption. Please be reminded that if anyone in your household travels overnight outside the Triangle our COVID policies may apply. In this case, your student will either need to stay virtual for 10 days or have a PCR lab test (NOT a rapid test) on Day 5 after return.  Please reach out to me on any related matters.
