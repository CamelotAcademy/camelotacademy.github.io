BEHIND THE SCENES:

My epiphany this week was how emotionally fulfilling it has been to have the whole student body together every day - within sight and sound of each other.  Although we have always been housed together in our one building - and have enjoyed the benefit of much togetherness - these opening weeks in Camelot Village have brought our connection to new levels.  Coming off of a year in which at times our school had more than 150 satellite sites, this time outside together has been an antidote of sorts to powerfully reconnect us all.  I am continually awed by the way students and teachers “go with the flow,” take care of each other and contribute their ideas and energies to the greater good.

 

REMINDERS

What does a Negative COVID test result mean?

A negative test result on any given day is a snapshot of that moment in time.  As the “small print” on all test reports indicate, sometimes a test can be negative in someone who has not yet progressed to a level of illness sufficient to be detected - effectively yielding a “false negative.” In the event that your student feels ill - even later the same day as a test was run - the possibility of COVID should be ruled out - usually by seeking another test.

A Message From the National Honor Society (NHS)

Hello Camelot students and families! Every October, the National Honor Society hosts a community drive called Socktober. Socks are one of the most under-donated items to homeless shelters, and they are one of the easiest to donate. To participate, all you have to do is bring new, unused socks to school and put them on the porch in the box labelled 'Socktober.' Of course, anything helps, so if you'd like to donate toothbrushes, paper towels, hair combs, or any other commonly donated item to homeless shelters, will gladly accept these items. During the Academic Fair, we'll announce the amount of socks donated to the school. Happy donating!
