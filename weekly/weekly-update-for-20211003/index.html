Weekly Update for 10/03/2021

Sunday, October 03, 2021

Greetings!

 

Congratulations to SR MAPP teacher Anna (Buchanan) Fields and her husband Sam as they welcome Eloise Lilly Fields to the world!  Eloise was born in the early morning of September 27.  The family is home and doing well.  Please feel free to send your congratulatory wishes and join their meal train:

https://mealtrain.com/2v0nd8

 

BEHIND THE SCENES:

It was a very successful post-retreat week.  By Tuesday the “hum” of students and teachers engaged and thriving in an established rhythm was palpable - always an exciting milestone in the launch of a school year!

 

REMIND:

Parent Night is this Thursday, October 7. 

You will receive an email with a Zoom link for your division(s) this week.

-         JR and SR MAPP - 5:30 PM

-         Middle Grades - 6:30 PM

-         Upper School - 7:30 PM

Please note this is a REQUIRED parent event.  Our agenda includes covering all general info - for each class - so that when we convene for Family Conferences the week of October 18 we can spend our time solely on exchanging information about your student and not on whole-class protocols or expectations.

 

 

ANNOUNCE:

Although our Monday COVID surveillance testing was “all clear,” on Friday we received two positive results in our Upper School (Gold) student body.  Of note, both students are fully vaccinated, have no symptoms and have different daily schedules.  Upon re-testing, one student has tested negative and the other is still awaiting results.  We are working with the NC Strong School team and the NCDHHS to determine if we are in receipt of a “false positive” result.  In the meantime, all families with a student who could have had a “close contact” (e.g. lunch group) with either student have been notified. We will update everyone on this list with the final status of both students when we have it.

 

If we have partial or full rain days this week, JR and SR MAPP students will have class on the porch. Middle and Upper School Students will be rotating inside to classrooms with open windows and physically-distanced seating.  We will use the outdoors for all students as much as feasible both on campus and at the farm.  If you prefer that your Middle Grades or Upper School students not rotate inside at all, please consider making arrangements for them to work from home for part or all of the day (based on what the weather actually brings us this week).

 

Module 3 begins tomorrow (Monday) which means a new slate of specials classes for MAPP and Middle Grades students.  For Upper School Purple-Track students it means the start of their Odd-numbered module long-block class (a science or social studies class).

 

I will stay in touch regarding COVID updates and I look forward to seeing you on Zoom on Thursday evening.

 

Best,

Thelma
