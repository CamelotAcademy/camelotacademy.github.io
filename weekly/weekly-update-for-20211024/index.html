Weekly Update for 10/24/2021

Sunday, October 24, 2021

Greetings!

 

Thank you for a productive round of Family Conferences.  It was a particularly worthwhile set of exchanges that will allow us to fine-tune our work together for optimal results and happiness. After such a focused week of interactions, I find my head and heart full with the hopes of each family and with ideas of how to best accomplish them. We appreciate your time, care and continued input. 

 

Would you please take a few minutes to complete this Post-Conference Survey?

Behind the Scenes:

 

The book From What Is to What If by Rob Hopkins has captured the imagination of a few of us - including Mr. Thomas who has been sharing excerpts with his classes.  If you are seeking a hopeful and empowering read about the power of imagination, I suggest it!

 

 

Announce:

 

Standing ovation for Ms. Erin Sullivan, our drama teacher for the past year.  She and her husband have made the decision to move to Wilmington, NC so that they can be closer to family and so she can accept an opportunity as Hospitality Coordinator at The Wilson Center Performing Arts Venue and the Wilma W. Daniels Art Gallery at Cape Fear Community College. Of course, Ms. Erin will be greatly missed in our daily routines, but we are looking forward both to her return as a guest artist and to offering school trips to Wilmington later this year for her to give some “behind the scenes” opportunities to our students at her new venue.  Meanwhile, we are excited to announce that Katie Rebich will be joining our team as the drama teacher.  Katie has an amazing background both as a performer and a teacher and we are delighted for our students to engage in some of the unique areas of instruction that she will be able to bring to our classes.  This week, Ms. Erin will officially be taking her bows and orienting Ms. Katie to her new role.  We look to our Camelot Family to help ease the transition for all involved.

 

Remind:

Our next round of COVID surveillance testing will be Monday (tomorrow) afternoon to compensate for no classes last Friday.  We will then resume the weekly testing on Friday morning.  Also tomorrow being Monday, we will have parking lot screening.  We are overjoyed that so many families were able to travel and gather over the past week (and to Zoom us in to exciting places for conferences!).  Of course, we ask for the continued vigilance of all when it comes to staying home when sick and/or taking proper precautions after an exposure.

 

As you know, an increasing amount of each school day is being spent indoors.  As the weather permits, our outside classrooms will be utilized, as well.  All snack and lunch breaks will continue to be outside. As such, this is the time of year to dress in layers since temperatures can vary by more than 20 degrees over the course of a school day. (Name labels on the layers would help keep the Lost and Found from overflowing!)

 

 

We are looking forward to being back together this week and putting our planning with you into practice.  Take good care!

 

Best,

 

Thelma
