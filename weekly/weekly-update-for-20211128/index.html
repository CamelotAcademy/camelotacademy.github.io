Weekly Update for 11/28/2021

Sunday, November 28, 2021

Greetings!

 

I hope your family enjoyed a wonderful Thanksgiving. It was lovely to kick the break off with our windy -  but delightful - gathering at the Academic Fair. We are very proud of the work of our students and very appreciative of the support our families provided to make the day a success. Special thanks to Ms. Magda who worked with our international students on their Academic Fair projects - and accomplished all the feats of shepherding projects AND did so with students whose first language is not English! My oversight in calling out her name at the event is beyond fathomable!  My heartfelt apologies.

 

This is an exciting time of year and the next three weeks, which I refer to as the “Interlude,” hold more than their share of activity.  Please pay extra attention to these Sunday Updates to keep informed.

 

Celebrate:

Congratulations to senior Nelle Poehlman for kicking off the college acceptances for the Class of ‘22 with an acceptance to the University of Missouri!

 

Kudos to NHS students for their work a few Saturdays ago helping to plant 350 daffodil bulbs at nearby Orchard Park - Max Terry, Eli Terry, Dylan McGlinn, Nelle Poehlman, Ian Powrie, Sam Pence, Dominic Harding and Pengyu QIan.  Special thanks to parents Paul Mastrapolo and Mary Bennett Poehlman for supervising the activity.

Announce:

 

COVID testing at Academic Fair once again produced 0% positivity!

 

This week we have COVID testing on Monday (tomorrow) afternoon (12:30-3) and on Friday morning, as usual.  Our vigilance in adhering to COVID protocols throughout the holiday period should help us achieve our goal of running school - and classes - without interruption. Since we will continue to take advantage of fresh air as much as possible, and since temperatures are forecasted to vary greatly, please continue to have students dress for the weather - including using LAYERS and labeling items with the owner's name. Please be sure to keep your students home if they are sick or if anyone in the household is exhibiting signs of illness or has had a known exposure. If in doubt, please check in with me as to the circumstances and please consider using an at-home test (available at most drugstores) on Monday morning if your week included airport travel or other activities that may have increased your exposure risk. If your student is home on Monday, or any COVID testing day, they may drive through our parking lot to participate in the testing - please just pre-arrange this with me. On behalf of our entire Camelot Team, let me say that we are deeply appreciative of your ongoing commitment, despite the inconveniences that sometimes occur.  

 

Starting this week, Middle Grades students will have increased practice in using their planners in each class, every day.  As you pack their belongings for school tomorrow please ensure the planner is included, if they have one.  If they wish to obtain one we will issue those on Monday.  Also starting this week, Middle Grades students still on campus at 3:30 PM will join after school study hall.  This will allow them a 45 minute recess before settling in to tackle homework.

 

 

I look forward to seeing the students and staff tomorrow and sharing stories from Academic Fair and the Break. To all those celebrating Hanukkah, we wish you a happy holiday.

 

Best,

 

Thelma
