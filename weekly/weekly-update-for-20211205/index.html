BEHIND THE SCENES:

It finally came to me over the Thanksgiving Break as to how we could maintain the tradition of a whole school picture for our holiday card while also maintaining COVID protocols.  Thanks to Greg, Greg’s drone, many teachers who were quick to mark out letters with orange cones and “think again” as to execution, the students and faculty on site on Tuesday, and the artistry of Mr. Irfan and Mr. Ab we have a special card coming!  The entire process was an example of teamwork at its best - full of fits and starts - with an end result that is more JOY-ful than any one of us could have imagined or executed on our own!

COVID RESULTS:

Our Tuesday COVID testing came back 0% positive and our Friday testing came back with 1 positive test - in a Middle Grades student.  There are also fourteen test results yet to come in because of a clerical error made by the technicians in submission.   The Middle Grades student was last on campus on Friday, was (and is) asymptomatic and is awaiting retest results.  We are not aware of any close contacts at this time but I will reach out later today with an update to all classmates in Cohorts 3 and 4.  Meanwhile, Ms. Lori has been in touch with the lab about the missing test results and we expect them later today.  Of course, if there is any info in those results that warrant communication, I will send out another email. Perhaps most importantly, we have worked with the lab to put things in place to ensure these complications do not happen again.

Our Middle Grades students have spent some of their Wednesday farm time helping to create new hugel beds for the upcoming season. This innovative approach to creating nutrient rich beds that are water efficient has been a boon to our gardening efforts and are a  focus of much interest.  To that end, Mr. Josh will host a parent farm visit this Saturday from 10:30-11:30 for anyone who would like to observe the process of creating the new beds. We have had a great first week back from break, although peppered with many absences due to non-COVID illness, possible exposures, etc.  We would like to make our re-entry from the even-longer Winter Break as smooth as possible for our families and teachers.  We will all be eager for a January return to a focused classroom environment as the semester will be wrapping up and exams and projects will be due. 

With all of this in mind, we have set up these plans for our return from Winter Break:

-         COVID testing will be offered in our parking lot on SUNDAY, January 2, from 1-4 PM.  Students who cannot attend should make alternate arrangements for a PCR test with results returned to me by 3 PM Tuesday.  (Wake Toxicology (in Durham) has a fast turnaround time.)  Home tests are not acceptable for this purpose.

-         Classes resume on TUESDAY, January 4 - BUT WILL BE VIRTUAL FOR ALL GRADE LEVELS (K-12) - THIS IS NEW INFO! Attendance is required of all students.

-         On campus classes resume WEDNESDAY, January 5 - PCR results must be provided prior to return.  NOTE - Only positive COVID test results/exposures will count as an excused absence. “Virtual” attendance for other circumstances will not be available.

On a related note: If your student’s COVID vaccination status on file needs updating, please do so as follows:

(1)  Log in to Achieve: https://cmlt.achievesms.us/

(if have problems, use the link on the login site to get help:  Support Email: achieve@camelotacademy.org)

(2) Select from Left Menu: Student/Household

(3) Select Questions Tab … The COVID Questions are the bottom 3 questions …

 

Thanks for your attention to all of this important info!  I hope you have a great week!

Best,

Thelma
