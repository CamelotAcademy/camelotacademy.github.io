-         Updates from the National Honor Society (NHS):

Camelot NHS is raising money for a couple of causes this Spring:

1.  Several community members are participating in the Durham Crop Walk.  Join us at 2pm Sunday, April 10th in front of the Duke Chapel.  You can walk by signing up here (https://events.crophungerwalk.org/2022/team/camelot-academy

scroll down to find the “Register” button .  You can also support the Camelot team financially by donating directly on the same link: https://events.crophungerwalk.org/2022/team/camelot-academy . Feel free to share with friends and family.

Camelot has been the 1st or 2nd highest fundraising school in the area for the last several years and we want to make our school proud again.

 

2.  We are also raising money for the UNC Hospital summer camp that supports kids with hemiplegia: https://www.uncchildrens.org/uncmc/unc-childrens/care-treatment/therapy/hemiplegia-camp/  Our Class of ‘22 graduate, Nelle Poehlman, had a stroke in utero and as a results has hemiplegia.   Hemiplegia (sometimes called hemiparesis) is a condition, caused by a brain injury, that results in a varying degree of weakness, stiffness (spasticity) and lack of control in one side of the body.   People with disabilities in general are sometimes looked down upon or are made fun of so we are fundraising for the camp and working for visibility and acceptance for all people.  NHS will match all the money we raise for the CROP Walk, up to $250, to donate to the camp that has meant so much to Nelle.  You can also contribute to this camp directly by sending change or money into Ms. Amy with a note that your donation is for The Helping Kids with Hemiplegia Camp.

 

This Saturday, April 9, is our final Open House (10 AM Zoom) of the year.  In addition to letting your friends know about this convenient way to learn about our school and meet our faculty, we encourage current 4th and 7th/8th graders to attend to learn about the “next division” (i.e. Middle and Upper School/high school) in their Camelot journey.  A Zoom link will be sent later this week. I am often reminded that there is much to the Camelot repertoire that even “our own” families have yet to discover!
