Weekly Update for 05/15/2022

Sunday, May 15, 2022

Greetings!

 

Thank you to everyone for making Thursday’s Evening of the Arts a joyful celebration of our students and our community.  As I looked out over the Camelot Family spread on the lawn with a background track of bird song and train whistles I was struck to realize our Family has not all been together in one place at one time since the 2019 Thanksgiving Feast.  No wonder my heart ached so much wanting the lift that is unique to such a gathering.  What a lovely memory we made!

 

BEHIND THE SCENES:

Here’s a brief retelling of how this week rolled... 

After I had a sleepless Sunday night trying to figure out a good plan for Evening of the Arts in light of incoming variables, on Monday morning my dog Dux and I took a walk on Camelot’s far-lawn.  In the midst of the walk Dux turned and looked at me meaningfully as if to say, “This is a good spot! - do it here!” I looked out at the lawn and could imagine it filled with families enjoying their children’s music and creativity.  As I walked back to the building Ms. Lori was in the carport. True to our treasured duet of many years, we collaborated on the idea and started thinking through logistics.  An hour later, on his first break from classes, Mr. Irfan walked the yard with us to figure out how we might run our electrical/technical needs to the location without creating havoc back at the building.  Tyler was instrumental in preparing the grounds and setting up the site. By Wednesday he was carrying enough of a To-Do list (with two long columns - one for the Camelot lawn and one for the Armory) that he was ready for anything! Ms. Wendy had the all-important assignment of finding staging and chairs - and spent a full day talking to vendors who were practically laughing at our last minute request at the height of graduation and wedding season - she prevailed and secured what we needed.  Meanwhile, Katie - the orchestrator of all the programming and our theater teacher - brought her Can-Do spirit to the plan and even volunteered to be the “voice of the location decision” if rain did us in - talk about being willing to “take one for the team!” Ms. Sasha, our music teacher, creatively led all her rehearsals in spite of a backdrop of complications that limited her access. Ms. Raleigh, our Middle/Upper School art teacher (who also designed our beautiful T-shirt this year), set up the third planned version of the student art display, this one on the porch.  She devoted uncompromised attention to showcasing each piece. Ms. Amy did a great job adapting the NHS ceremony to work beautifully in the setting.  All of this was supported by every staff person working a super-long day pitching in wherever and however needed and students of all ages eagerly and excitedly doing the same. When the 2 PM weather forecast remained dicey, it was the this enthusiasm to make it happen on campus that informed the decision to “GO FOR IT!’  The Behind the Scenes story this week is as heart-felt as the event itself and my gratitude to our community runs deep. We gave our Class of ‘22 a sweet memory to take away as their final gathering with the whole community.
