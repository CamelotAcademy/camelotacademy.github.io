Update From the National Honor Society:

First off, we’ve done a great job so far with our community service this year - thank you to everyone for your support!

-We raised over $300 dollars for Homeless Veterans at our Chipotle fundraiser last month

-We got all of the competition fields put together and sent to the NC Lego Robotics League

- We learned that Camelot Academy won the Golden Sneaker award for last year’s Cropwalk!

In Socktober news, we're getting close to our goal of 200 pairs of socks! If we can collect 75 more before the Academic Fair, we will celebrate with a whole-school cookie event.

 

Our candy drive is also ongoing - all extra Halloween candy you bring to the collection box on the porch will go to local Ronald McDonald houses.

 

On Tuesday, NHS  is sponsoring a Kids Voting Durham mock election, coordinated by senior Annabel Swansey. Kids Voting Durham (KVD) is a nonpartisan organization which encourages youth civic participation by holding mock elections with real candidates. KVD also publishes a candidate guide which is helpful for both kids and adults to make informed voting choices. You can access it here online: https://www.kidsvotingdurham.org/2022-eg#3vid
