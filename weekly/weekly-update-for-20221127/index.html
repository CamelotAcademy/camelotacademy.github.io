Weekly Update for 11/27/2022

Sunday, November 27, 2022

Greetings!

 

Congratulations to senior Dylan McGlinn who earned the first college acceptance of the Class of ‘23 with an Early Decision acceptance to High Point University, accompanied by a 48 K Merit Scholarship!
 

Thank you for the excellent participation in Academic Fair. It was a beautiful event on all fronts - a showcase of student interests and research, an opportunity for them to share their musical gifts and a much-desired time for parent socializing.  I hope the “feel good” vibe set your holiday week off on a positive note.

 

Behind the scenes:

We now enter the three week period of time between holiday breaks that I call Interlude.  These weeks are packed with special activities and full of anticipation of holiday plans to come.

 

Remind:

During this extra busy time of year it is important that we all heed our policies on parking lot safety. Although the staggered drop off and pick up times are a huge help, we still have everything from small children to student drivers sharing space and time. 

-         Please drop off or pick up your student only from the designated spot (by the tent). 

-         Please drive through the lot SLOWLY. 

-         Please park for conversations or other needs that can tie up the carpool line.                                     

Thank you for contributing to this gift of safety and courtesy to our community.

 

THIS FRIDAY (December 2):  Upper School (Purple and Gold)  early dismissal:

-         Gold - 11:30 dismissal for all

-         Purple - 12:00 OPTIONAL dismissal (music students should stay for music class, others who opt to stay will have study hall.)

 

Announce:

 

We officially welcome Logan Seay to our administrative team.  Logan will be onboarding during this Interlude period and learning about our students, schedules, programs and policies to support the administrative side of our work.

 

Ms. Charlotte has put together a Google Classroom called Forge Your Path which is full of opportunities for high school students - including summer programs, travel, GAP year and internship opportunities.  Check it out regularly for newly posted info.  All Upper School students have received an invitation but they can also join with the code: 26nkxga

 

In one week (week of December 5) the Yearbook Class will sponsor a Spirit Week.  See below for details and to plan your attire!

 

Driver’s Ed info for Camelot from DPS:

Registration for all non-Durham Public Schools students will happen on Saturday, Dec. 3rd at 9:00am.  Students will go to the website dpsnc.net, go to program/services/scroll down to registration.  Classes will begin on January 5th, 2023.  Students can register for the morning class which will be from 6:30 am to 8:30 am or for the evening class which will run from 4:30 pm to 6:30 pm.  Students will be required to attend all fifteen days of class.  No absences are allowed. Students may only register for one of the classes.  Registration will be limited and once the capacity has been reached the registration will shut down.  Once a student is chosen for the class they will receive an email with all the details.  All non DPS students must have a Gmail account in order to be in the class.  Robin Derr will be the instructor for both of these classes. Please reach out to her if you have any questions.   robin_derr@dpsnc.net

 

COVID Update:

- We had ZERO positive tests on Thursday, November 17.

- This week, we will have testing tomorrow (Monday) and on Friday (resuming regular schedule).

- Durham County CDC level is LOW (weekly update as of 11/24/22.  https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/your-health/covid-by-county.html)

 

I hope you have a great week.

 

Best,

Thelma
