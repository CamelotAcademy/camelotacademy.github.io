Weekly Update for 01/15/2022

Sunday, January 15, 2023

Greetings!

 

Congratulations to these seniors on their recent good news:

-         Gabriella Taylor on her acceptance to UNC Greensboro

-         Annabel Swansey on her awards from the National Scholastic Art Award competition - she received 2 Silver Keys and 2 Honorable Mentions

 

 

Behind the scenes:

With the first semester a wrap, the teachers and I now enter an intensive period of writing comments and producing report cards.  The amount of care and hours that go into this process is significant and - of course - this all takes place while school is in session!   We are excited to share student progress with you, with many enhancements to the format.

 

 

Remind:

 

School Closed Tomorrow – Monday, January 16 – in observance of MLK Jr Day.

 

Upper School X Schedule begins on Tuesday and runs through January 27. 

-         Animal Behavior students arrive at 8:30 AM

-         All Gold classes run until 3 PM (no early dismissals)

 

The Second Semester begins for ALL on Tuesday!

 

This week is the last regularly scheduled week of “Fall” Clubs. 

-         Chess Club has a make-up session on Monday, Jan. 23

-         Foraging Club has a make-up session on Thursday, Jan 26

 

 

 

Announce:

 

We are very excited to be hosting a series of career speakers on Tuesday and Wednesday for our Upper School students.  Thanks to Ms. Charlotte for setting it up and to the many parents contributing their time and expertise.

 

Wednesday - Basketball Knights have a game @ Fletcher Academy  4:00 PM,  (400 Cedarview Ct., Raleigh)

 

 

COVID Update:

We had ZERO positive tests on Friday plus ONE new positive during the week (faculty). There are FOUR tests pending.

 

All students should carry a mask to use if needed, every day this week.  Classes with guest speakers or those attending field trips at indoor locations may be required to wear a mask. Masks remain highly recommended for everyone inside the building in light of our CDC County status of HIGH.  We continue to monitor community and school metrics and will adjust our protocols if/as needed.

 

Durham County CDC level is HIGH (weekly update as of 1/12/23.  https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/your-health/covid-by-county.html )

 

Thank you to all of you for the proactive communication - always, but especially over the past week. Our staff greatly appreciates your vigilance as they have exciting X Schedule plans to execute and demanding report writing expectations in the coming days.

 

Please continue to be vigilant about:

-         Not sending a student sick with any contagious illness to school.

-         Notifying me if anyone in your household tests positive for COVID or if your student has had a known exposure to COVID. This will allow us to set up a schedule to monitor with antigen tests at school but this will NOT keep your student from attending school.

 

 

I hope you have a great week!

 

Best,

Thelma
