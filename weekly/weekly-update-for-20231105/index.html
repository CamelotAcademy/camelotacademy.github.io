Weekly Update for 11/05/2023

Sunday, November 05, 2023

Greetings!

 

Happy November!  I hope you enjoy the extra hour of the weekend!

 

Congratulations to Senior Mattie Ramont who starts off our college announcements for the Class of ‘24.   Mattie was accepted to Susquehanna University with a Trustee Scholarship of $168,000.

 

Welcome Amy Shields to our faculty.  Amy has moved here from New Zealand and brings a wealth of experience from her time in day and boarding schools in other parts of the world.  She is filling in across the grade levels right now as an introduction to our school.  In January she will be covering Anna’s maternity leave.

 

Behind the scenes:

The passage of time last week was a bit warped from my lens.  The week started with summer, moved to winter, ended with fall.  We had a holiday, a major college deadline and the release of mid-semester reports.  Meanwhile, all those who teach English were consumed with reading and editing hundreds of pages of Academic Fair rough draft papers.  Such are the things that can distort the sense of time in the Land of Camelot…..and then the calendar ADDS an hour of time back in….and all is well!

 

Remind:

Tuesday is PICTURE DAY.  Individual student portraits and the whole school picture will be taken.  Senior portraits, sports and club pictures, etc. are taken in the spring.

 

Mid-Semester Reports are available to view on the Achieve Portal through next weekend.  If you received an email with a Mid-Semester Warning, please send an acknowledgement email by Friday.

 

Announce:

Presentation and Performance times for the Academic Fair (FRIDAY, November 17) will be sent out later this week.       

-         MIDDLE and UPPER SCHOOL students will be assigned a 25 minute presentation rotation.

-         MAPP and UPPER SCHOOL music students will be performing mid-morning

The event runs from 8 AM until NOON at the Marriott Convention Center and is a required parent and student event, K-12.  (Families may attend just during their allotted time slots or stay for the full event.)

 

 

Wellness Update:

We have colds and some GI woes among us.  All returning from illness have had an antigen test - no positive COVID tests and no known positive tests for Flu, Strep or RSV. 

 

REMINDER:

Per the Family Handbook and Wellness policy:

Students are to stay home if in the last 24 hours they have:

o   Run a fever over 100 F (without meds)

o   Had an undiagnosed rash

o   Had symptoms of GI or respiratory infection such as cough, sore throat, vomiting or diarrhea.

Please report symptoms and absences by using the dedicated email address:  ATTENDANCE@CAMELOTACADEMY.ORG.

 

I hope you have a great week!

 

Best,

Thelma

Academic Fair at the Marriott Convention Center (downtown Durham).

- This is a whole-school required parent event - no classes on campus

- Dismissal at noon for Fall Break
