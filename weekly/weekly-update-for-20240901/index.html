Weekly Update for 09/01/2024

Sunday, September 01, 2024

Greetings!

 

We had a super launch to the year thanks to our amazing team and the winning spirit of our students. Special thanks to Lori, Michelle, and Nicole who orchestrated the orientation programming for the Upper, Middle and MAPP divisions, respectively. Logan, Wendy and Jill were - as usual - invaluable in more ways than one can count.

 

 

BEHIND THE SCENES:

Our Friday afternoon full faculty meeting found us exhausted, hot and exuberant about the potential of this student body.  We swapped stories of WONDER-full moments from the opening sequence and put finishing touches on plans for the coming weeks.  Now, for a well-earned and much-needed long weekend rest!

 

During my Thursday small group rotation with all Upper School students we began the dialogue both about whey they WONDER about and also about what would make this a fantastic school year.  Each student wrote responses on a notecard which was turned in to me and now provides additional fodder for our journey together.  Please feel free to extend this dialogue to the home-front!

 

REMIND:

 

School is closed Monday in observance of Labor Day.

 

Lunch orders are due each Wednesday by midnight for the upcoming week.

 

SPAIN TRIP INFO ZOOM - Wednesday at 7 PM

Spain: The Grand Tour

(open to Middle Grades students with an accompanying adult and Upper School students- with or without an adult.)

Our 10-day tour will run June 10-19, 2025 and we'll visit Madrid, Seville, Granada, and Barcelona! The itinerary includes special experiential activities: a cooking class, a Spanish Civil War role-play, a Flamenco dance lesson, and a study of Spanish artists. Plus, participants may earn high school or college credit if they complete an optional online course after their trip.

There will be an interest meeting via Zoom this Wednesday, Sept. 4 at 7pm to expand on the information already provided and answer questions you may have. (Note: You do not need to complete the 'I'm interested' box on the website to attend the interest meeting.)

See more about this trip here: educationaltravel.com/Frysinger-4455

FORGE YOUR PATH OPPORTUNITIES:

 

Teen Volunteer Opportunity: DPAC Performing Arts Ambassadors

Ambassadors shadow staff across multiple departments and attend a wide variety of DPAC events. Report on experiences via videos, online blogs, and social media posts. Each month, Ambassadors will self-select the schedule for the performances that work with their pre-existing school, family and other commitments. This is a 9-month commitment. Apply soon! Due Monday, Sept. 9

https://www.dpacnc.com/community/performing-arts-ambassadors

 

 

2024 Congressional App Challenge

This challenge is open to Middle and High School students! Whether you are a beginner in coding or a seasoned developer, the challenge provides an excellent platform to put your skills to the test, gain valuable experience, and connect with other students from across NC’s 4th Congressional District. The CAC is designed to empower students to develop original apps that address real-world issues or explore their passion for technology. A Jumpstarter event will take place on Saturday, September 21st at IBM in RTP (registration required).
