Weekly Update for 04/27/2025

Sunday, April 27, 2025

Greetings!

 

Last week was short on school days but BIG on activity!  Kudos to Lori for coordinating State testing and all of its intricacies. She was marvelously supported by the diligent efforts of the full team.

 

APPLAUSE to Katie who orchestrated a fun and entertaining Talent Show.  Kudos to Irfan for the tech support. The weather cooperated with a beautiful spring day and the opportunity for everyone from our “littles” to our seniors to gather on the lawn to laugh, clap, hoot and holler and take the time to enjoy each other.

 

 

BEHIND THE SCENES:

Wow! What a faculty work day we had on Friday!  The INDIVIDUALITY and FLEXIBILITY of our programming was at the forefront of our detail-driven planning sessions. Much fun ahead!

 

 

REMIND:

 

Juniors and their parents (Class of ‘26) have Tuesday at 7PM Zoom meeting with me to review our work in Junior Meetings and to set summer expectations. Zoom link to follow with a reminder on Tuesday.

 

Soccer Club has wrapped up for the season.  Kudos to Coach Scott and the players whose hard work gives us much to anticipate going forward.

 

 

ANNOUNCE:

 

Friday is Evening Of the Arts!

Location: Hayti Heritage Center (804 Old Fayetteville St. - 5 minutes from Camelot)

-         This WHOLE SCHOOL, Parent-Required Event is a joyful celebration of our arts programming!

-         Student performers arrive by 5:45 PM (includes all MAPP, Middle, Upper School Theater and Music classes and the Class of ‘25! )

-         Performance 6- 8 PM

-         Please allow time to find street parking and walk to the venue

-         ALL STUDENTS SHOULD WEAR THEIR NEW CAMELOT T-SHIRTS (being sent home Friday afternoon).

-         In addition to all of the on-stage performances, the gallery will be brimming with activity: K-12 Student artwork display, student craft sales, Egg and Honey sale, beaded item fundraiser, Summer Program Info.

 

 

Wednesday, 12:30-1:30 -  OPTIONAL Junior SAT Prep workshop with Reed.

 

HOST FAMILIES NEEDED:

We are still seeking host families for three of our international high school students.  Would your family be interested in hosting an international high school student for a semester or two?  If you can offer a private bedroom, transportation to/from school and meals and would be interested in this wonderful opportunity for cultural exchange, please let me know.  Host families receive a monthly stipend to cover room, board and transportation costs.

 

I hope you have a great week and I look forward to seeing you Friday night!

 

Best,

Thelma
