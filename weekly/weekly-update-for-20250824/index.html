Weekly Update for 08/24/2025

Sunday, August 24, 2025

Greetings!

Welcome to the first installment of our Weekly Updates for the 2025-26 school year.  Think of this as a weekly message sent with much care to keep us all on the same page as we go on this journey together on behalf of the young people we all cherish. I will send you an email each Sunday morning throughout the year.  Please make the careful reading of this message part of your routine at a convenient time prior to end of day Monday in order to get the most traction from the info. Thank you, in advance!

Good News:

We are excited to welcome our students to campus this week and to see all parents on Zoom on Monday.  We’ve planned, prepped, painted, potted…even as I fine-tune this message this Sunday morning there are early risers on campus making magic….and now we are eager and ready to launch this new year.

BEHIND THE SCENES:

Last week we welcomed many new students, student ambassadors and members of the Class of ‘26 to campus.  Kudos to theater teacher Katie for planning and hosting three very successful days of “soft orientation” for our newest students. We also hosted three family gatherings at Lakewood Social and from all accounts that schedule addition is a “keeper.” Many thanks to our admin team - Wendy, Logan, Amy S and Amy C for all of the background and front-of-house work in our multi-layered launch sequence that opens a new school year.

Also, special thanks to Lori, Charlotte, Leah, Rachel and our building and grounds team of Greg, Tyler and Josh for all of the attention to details large and small to create a welcoming backdrop to our activities.

We wrapped up our Teacher Work Days on Friday with a baby shower for Sarah.  She and her husband Zach are expecting a baby girl In late September.  It was lovely to gather in joyous anticipation and Michelle did a beautiful job orchestrating our colleagues to participate in the surprise.

REMIND:

If you have not already, please watch the Parent Orientation pre-recorded info before attending Monday’s live Zoom meetings. Kudos to Amy C for coordinating the scripts and to Irfan for filming and editing it all.  We hope this hybrid format allows you to partake when the time is most convenient and to focus on the info that is most relevant to you.

NEWS:

Over the summer veteran teacher Tom Mitchell had some new opportunities emerge aligned with his many talents and passions outside the classroom. He has decided to pursue these avenues and we wish him all the best and look forward to his visits and updates in the coming months.

Other recent news is that Magda Sledzik Sims will be on medical leave for the first part of the school year.  We have created a schedule that allows for her return to some of her favorite things, such as Linguistics and Model UN, later in the year. We look forward to having Magda’s wonderful presence back in our midst soon and know you join us in sending her well wishes. Meanwhile, we are honored to welcome Christine Donovan Ball to our ELL teaching team. Christine brings many years of experience to this task and is eager to meet and support our international students.

ANNOUNCE:

 

Ultimate Frisbee Event- Saturday, September 6

DISCover Ultimate Day for beginners/first time players

,

Triangle Ultimate is hosting DISCover Ultimate Day, a free, two-hour clinic designed specifically for middle and high school-aged players who are new to the sport. This event is part of a national initiative led by USA Ultimate to introduce more young people to the game. This is a great, low-pressure way for kids to learn what ultimate is all about through fun games, basic skills, and team-building activities.

Event Details

 September 6th

 9:00am - 11:00am

 Pleasant Park, Apex or Piney Wood Park, Durham

 Free for all participants

 Geared toward beginners / first-time players

 DISCover Ultimate event page: https://www.triangleultimate.org/e/2025-discover-ultimate

 

FAMILY EVENT - Star-gazing at the farm!- Friday, September 12

For all aspiring astronomers or casual star enthusiasts, Mr. Thomas will be hosting an all ages star-gazing opportunity at the Camelot Farm on Friday, 09/12 from 7:00-9:30pm. This is a beginner-friendly event, and we will spend some time identifying basic constellations, discussing the moon, and Mr. Thomas will have a telescope set up.

Enrollment is limited to 12 carloads (due to parking limitations).  Students grades K-7 must be accompanied by a parent.  Parents of all age students are welcome and encouraged to attend.  Sign Up here: https://www.signupgenius.com/go/904054BADA62BA4FD0-58119465-camelot#/
